Our Recommended Crime TV Shows

This is a small personal selection of crime TV shows we have watched and recommend, chosen for their characters as much as their cases. Each series approaches the genre differently, but all of them are driven by people, not just plot.

Our picks

Three shows. Three different moods. All character-first.

1) Prodigal Son

2 seasons • Psychological crime • Intense

Prodigal Son is intense and a bit crazy, and that’s exactly why it works. It follows Malcolm Bright, a criminal profiler who understands killers a little too well. The reason is personal: his father is a famous serial murderer.

The cases can get dark, but the real hook is the relationship at the center. It’s tense, messy, and always on edge, and the show doesn’t pretend it’s healthy.

If you want a crime series that feels sharp, restless, and character-heavy, this one delivers.

2) Harrow

3 seasons • Medical crime drama

Harrow is a medical crime drama built around a forensic pathologist who keeps getting pulled into cases. It’s not trying to be flashy. It stays grounded and lets the situations feel real.

What I like here is the mood. It’s serious without being loud, and it keeps the focus on people — the victims, the families, the decisions, and the damage that follows.

If you want crime that feels more realistic and adult, Harrow is a solid watch.

3) The Mentalist

7 seasons • Crime procedural • Personal favourite

The Mentalist is the most fun pick here. It’s light, easy to watch, and often genuinely funny. Patrick Jane’s charm carries the show, and the cases move with a nice rhythm.

But it’s not just jokes. There are serious moments, and there’s a long emotional thread underneath that gives the whole series weight when it needs it.

This one is my comfort crime show — entertaining, rewatchable, and still capable of hitting hard at times.
